Details of Kunvarbai Nu Mameru Yojana


Kunvarbai no Mameru Yojana” has been running for a long time in the state of Gujarat for the welfare of the daughters of poor families and to help them financially. Daughters married in Kuvarbainu mameru yojna get assistance paid directly into their bank account through DBT. This scheme is given to girls belonging to Scheduled Castes, Socially and Educationally Backward Classes, Economically Weaker Families by marrying off their daughters. 12000/- (twelve thousand) rupees is directly deposited in the bank account through assistance in this scheme.

If a girl from an economically weak family of Gujarat state gets married, the Gujarat government used to pay 10,000/- (ten thousand rupees) assistance directly to the girl's bank account through DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer). which has been amended.

• A couple married after 01/04/2021 will get Rs 12,000/- (twelve thousand) under kuvarbai mameru yojana.
• Couples married before 01/04/2021 will be entitled to Rs 10,000/- (ten thousand) as per old resolution.

• The benefit of this scheme is available to scheduled castes (natives of Gujarat state) only
• The annual income limit in this scheme is Rs.600000/- in rural areas and ₹600000/- in urban areas.
• Benefit under this scheme till the marriage of two (2) adult girls of the family
• The age limit of the girl at the time of marriage should be 18 years and the age of the youth should be 21 years.
• Assistance must be applied for within two years of marriage.
• The assistance of the scheme will be available to women from seven Fera Group Scheduled Districts.

• Schemes will show the caste as indicated by the beneficiary at the time of registration. In which one has to go to the Kuvarbai Nu Mameru Yojana Online Form and fill the requested details and submit the online application.

  1. Daughter's Aadhaar Card
  2. Girl's Election Card (ID)
  3. Aadhaar card of the father of the beneficiary bride
  4. Gender pattern of girls
  5. Girl's School Leaving Certificate or Birth Certificate
  6. Annual income certificate of bride's father/guardian
  7. Residence proof of bride (any one of Licence/Electoral Card/Ration Card/Electricity Bill)
  8. Copy of first page of bride's bank passbook (with father's/guardian's name after bride's name)
  9. A joint photo of the bride and groom
  10. Groom's Date of Birth Aadhaar (L.C/Date of Birth Specimen/Government Doctor's Certificate in case of Illiterate)
  11. Marriage registration certificate
  12. Affidavit of bride's father/guardian
  13. Bond of bride's father
  14. Example of death if bride's father is not alive
